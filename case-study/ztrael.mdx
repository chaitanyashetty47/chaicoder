## The Challenge

Ztraél was already investing in paid marketing and driving steady traffic to its Shopify store, around **2,000-2,500 visitors per month**.

But despite this consistent acquisition, the **conversion rate hovered around ~1.8%**.

An audit of the landing and product page experience revealed that the issue wasn’t interest, it was friction:

* Broken and inconsistent CTAs disrupted the purchase flow.
* Heavy animations and visual effects caused lag, especially on mobile.
* Large, unoptimized images led to slow page loads.
* A visually cluttered layout made it harder for visitors to quickly understand value and move to checkout.

Ztraél didn’t need more traffic.

**They needed a faster, clearer, conversion-focused buying experience.**

---

## The Solution

We focused on **increasing conversion efficiency without changing the traffic sources**.

The redesign and engineering work prioritised speed, clarity, and a smoother path to purchase:

* Simplified the layout and visual hierarchy so the product story and value were immediately clear.
* Fixed and standardised CTAs to create a consistent, intuitive purchase path.
* Implemented lazy loading for images to reduce initial page load time.
* Added CDN-backed image optimisation and caching for snappier performance.
* Reduced unnecessary animations and heavy visual effects that were slowing down the interface.
* Refined the product page structure to better support decision-making (benefits, use-cases, and offer clarity before the add-to-cart step).

The goal wasn’t just a prettier store-it was a **faster, more focused product experience that turned existing attention into revenue**.

---

## Results

With **no change in traffic volume** (still around **~2,300 monthly visitors**), Ztraél saw:

* **Conversion rate uplift: 1.8% → 3.2%**
* **~78% increase in orders from the same traffic**
* **Higher revenue per visitor**, indicating stronger monetisation of existing ad spend

By fixing friction in the buying journey, the store began behaving like a true premium brand-not just in visuals, but in performance.

---

## Final Reflection

Ztraél didn’t need a new acquisition strategy.

**They needed their Shopify experience to work as hard as their marketing spend.**

By tightening performance, cleaning up the product journey, and aligning design with conversion principles, we helped Ztraél **unlock significantly more revenue from the traffic they were already paying for**.
